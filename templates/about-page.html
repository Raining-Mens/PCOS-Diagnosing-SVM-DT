Ovarian Disease Prediction Tool

This tool will predict if the patient is infected with Polycystic Ovary Syndrome or Ovarian Cancer by examining physical and clinical assessment of the patient using the machine learning models SVM and Decision Tree

ADASYN-ENN

ADASYN-ENN is an ensemble balancing method consists of Adaptive Synthetic and Edited Nearest Neighbor. Adaptive Synthetic is an over-sampling technique which generates synthetic instances on the region with less positive instances than one with more positive instances to increase the recognition of positive. On the other hand, ENN or Edited Nearest Neighbor is an under-sampling technique that deletes sample sets like noises which can be present after an over-sampling method was processed to the data. ENN is used to remove noisy data that cancel out the flaws of ADASYN, which may result on producing a much robust balanced data for better fitting into classifiers.

PRECISION-RECALL CURVE (DECISION TREE)

The graph below shows the precision-recall curve or PRC of decision tree algorithm with and without Adasyn-Enn. The three lines indicates the PRC of each classes and the average of the two. The solid black line corresponds to the class 0 which is the classes that don't have PCOS or Ovarian Cancer, the solid green line corresponds to the class 1, those that have PCOS or Ovarian Cancer and the dotted blue line is the average. While the PRC of class 0 is higher when there is no Adasyn-Enn, the PRC of class 1 is significantly higher if the Adasyn-Enn resulting to higher average PRC.
